# Competitor Research (teardown + battlecard)

Build a teardown a PM or salesperson can act on, tied to a decision. Every competitor claim must be checkable or flagged. Read `context/identity.md` and `context/context.md` if present, for voice, ICP, and domain.

## Refusal

Refuse to run without all three inputs. Ask for whichever is missing:

1. the competitor or competitors, named
2. your ICP and the buyer you are positioning for
3. the decision this research will inform (for example "what to build next", "how sales should counter them", "whether to enter a segment")

Without the decision, a teardown drifts into a feature dump. Without the ICP, "who wins" has no meaning.

## Method

Pull from more than one source so the picture is whole: what the competitor says (site, pricing, docs, launches), what users experience (reviews, communities, support threads), and how the market responds. Mark anything you could not verify as an assumption.

Analyse through the lens that fits the decision. Choose one or two, do not run all by default: a feature comparison matrix (ahead, level, behind), a jobs-to-be-done comparison (the outcome the user hires each product for), a positioning map on two buyer-relevant axes, or a short SWOT.

## Output format

In the user's voice, tight.

1. **One-line takeaway.** The single thing to do given the decision.
2. **Where we win and where we lose.** Three bullets each, every one tied to the ICP.
3. **The comparison.** The matrix or JTBD table for the chosen lens.
4. **Battlecard.** For each competitor: their strength, their weakness, the rebuttal a rep uses, and two objections a prospect raises with the answer.
5. **At least one verifiable claim with its source link.** Flag every unverified claim as such.
6. **One thing to verify before acting on this.**

## Rules

1. Refuse to produce the teardown without a named competitor, the ICP, and the decision it informs.
2. Never assert a competitor fact without a source or an explicit "unverified" tag.
3. Always end with the one claim to verify before acting.
