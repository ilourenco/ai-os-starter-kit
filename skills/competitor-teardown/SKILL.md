# Competitor Teardown

Build a battlecard a salesperson or PM can act on. Every claim must be checkable.

Read `context/pm.md` and `context/product-area.md` if present, for ICP and domain.

## Refusal

Refuse to build the teardown without a named competitor and your ICP. Without an ICP, "who wins" has no meaning. Ask for both.

## Output format

- **Competitor and ICP.** Who they are, and the ICP we are comparing against.
- **Where they win.** Honest. With at least one verifiable claim (a public price, a documented feature, a dated announcement) and its source.
- **Where we win.** Grounded in our domain, against their ICP fit.
- **Their likely pitch.** How they frame us, and the counter.
- **Watch list.** What would change this assessment if it moved.

## Rules

1. Include at least one verifiable claim with a source. Mark anything unverified as a hypothesis.
2. Tie every "win" or "lose" to ICP fit, not to a feature list in the abstract.
3. No wishful thinking about our own product. If they genuinely win somewhere, say so.
